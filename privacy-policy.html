Introduction

CalAIder ("we," "us," or "our") is a Chrome extension designed to help users capture event details from any webpage and manage their Google Calendar with AI-powered extraction. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your information when you use our extension.

By using CalAIder, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

Information We Collect

1. Personal Information

When you use CalAIder, we may collect the following personal information:

  • Google Account Information: We request access to your Google account to authenticate and manage your calendar events. This includes your email address, profile information, and calendar data.
  • Calendar Events: Information about events you create, edit, or delete through the extension, including event titles, dates, times, locations, and descriptions.

2. Usage Data

We may collect information about how you use the extension, including:

  • The webpages you visit while using CalAIder (only when you actively use the extension on that page)
  • The events you capture and manage through the extension
  • Feature usage patterns to improve the extension

3. Technical Data

We may automatically collect certain technical information, including:

  • Browser type and version
  • Extension version
  • Error logs and diagnostic information

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To Provide and Maintain Our Service: We use your information to enable the core functionality of the extension, including capturing event details from webpages and managing your Google Calendar.
  • To Improve Our Service: We analyze usage data to enhance the performance, features, and user experience of CalAIder.
  • To Provide Support: We may use your information to respond to your inquiries and provide customer support.
  • To Ensure Security: We use information to detect, prevent, and address technical issues and security vulnerabilities.

Data Processing and Storage

Local Processing: CalAIder uses Chrome's built-in AI (Gemini Nano) to process event extraction locally on your device. This means that webpage content is analyzed on your computer, not sent to external servers for processing.

Local Storage: Event data and user preferences are stored locally in your browser using Chrome's storage API. This data remains on your device and is not transmitted to our servers.

Google API Interaction: When you add, edit, or delete calendar events, CalAIder communicates directly with Google's Calendar API using secure OAuth 2.0 authentication. We do not store or have access to your Google account credentials.

Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your personal information to outside parties. We may share your information only in the following circumstances:

  • With Your Consent: We may share your information with third parties if you provide explicit consent.
  • With Google Services: Calendar data is shared with Google Calendar API to enable calendar management features. This interaction is governed by Google's Privacy Policy.
  • For Legal Reasons: We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities.
  • To Protect Rights: We may disclose information to protect our rights, privacy, safety, or property, and that of our users or the public.

Security of Your Information

We take the security of your personal information seriously and implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect it, including:

  • Using secure OAuth 2.0 authentication for Google account access
  • Processing sensitive data locally on your device when possible
  • Encrypting data transmissions using HTTPS
  • Following Chrome extension security best practices
  • Limiting extension permissions to only what is necessary for functionality

However, no method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

Your Rights and Choices

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access: You can access the personal information we hold about you by reviewing your calendar data through Google Calendar.
  • Correction: You can correct any inaccurate personal information by editing events directly in the extension or Google Calendar.
  • Deletion: You can delete your personal information by:
    • Removing the extension from Chrome
    • Revoking CalAIder's access to your Google account in your Google Account settings
    • Clearing extension data from Chrome's storage
  • Opt-Out: You can stop using the extension at any time by disabling or uninstalling it.

To exercise any of these rights or if you have questions about your data, please contact us using the information provided below.

Third-Party Services

CalAIder integrates with the following third-party services:

  • Google Calendar API: Used to read, create, edit, and delete calendar events. Subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Google OAuth 2.0: Used for secure authentication. Subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Chrome Built-in AI (Gemini Nano): Used for local event extraction. Processes data on your device only.

We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these third-party services. We encourage you to review their privacy policies.

Children's Privacy

CalAIder is not intended for use by children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so we can take appropriate action.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. We will notify you of any material changes by:

  • Posting the new Privacy Policy on this page
  • Updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy
  • Providing notice through the extension (for significant changes)

You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes. Changes to this Privacy Policy are effective when they are posted on this page.

Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to provide you with the service and fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy. Specifically:

  • Local data stored by the extension is retained until you clear it or uninstall the extension
  • OAuth tokens are cached by Chrome and can be revoked at any time
  • Calendar data is managed by Google Calendar and subject to their retention policies

California Privacy Rights

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including:

  • The right to know what personal information we collect about you
  • The right to request deletion of your personal information
  • The right to opt-out of the sale of your personal information (we do not sell personal information)
  • The right to non-discrimination for exercising your privacy rights

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information below.
